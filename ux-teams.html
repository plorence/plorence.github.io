UX Teams

I've managed a variety of UX teams in my career. Some teams I've inherited and expanded, while other teams I've built completely from scratch. Some teams have been completely local, other teams were distributed geographically. I've refined my player-coach management philosophy to both deal with the evolving UX function at agile start-up companies as well as incorporate great ideas from team members with diverse experience. I've done a lot of hiring (and a very small amount of firing). I've written plenty of performance reviews and had some tough performance management conversations.

In every company I've worked for, I've been asked to move from individual contributor into a management role. I've enjoyed each transition because I felt I'd gained the respect and recognition from my peer group prior to moving to a more strategic and managerial role. I've come to understand the dymanic between product management and engineering, and I've learned how to advocate for user-centered design practices as development teams become more agile.

I'm particularly proud of the UX team that I built at IronPort; several of the folks I hired reported to me for over 5 years. (All have moved on to continue to do great things!) This long and consistent UX support over the multiple releases and product lines helped establish the maturity of UX best practices throughout all phases of the development lifecycle during a very dynamic time at the startup.
